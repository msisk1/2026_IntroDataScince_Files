EPA Air Quality System (AQS) Data Documentation
Cuyahoga County, Ohio — Annual Monitor Summary, 2024
Accessed: June 15, 2026

OVERVIEW

This file (cuyahoga_aqs_2024.csv) contains annual air quality summary statistics for
monitoring stations operating in Cuyahoga County, Ohio during calendar year 2024. Data
were obtained from the EPA Air Quality System Pre-Generated Data Files, available at
https://aqs.epa.gov/aqsweb/airdata/download_files.html.

The source file (annual_conc_by_monitor_2024.zip) contains data for all U.S. monitoring
stations. This subset has been filtered to Cuyahoga County stations measuring PM2.5
and ozone only.

POLLUTANT AND AVERAGING PERIOD SELECTION

Two pollutants are included:

  PM2.5 (Fine Particulate Matter)
  Parameter name: PM2.5 - Local Conditions
  Sample duration: 24-HR BLK AVG (24-hour block average)
  Regulatory standard applied: PM25 24-hour 2024
  Units: Micrograms per cubic meter (ug/m3)
  EPA annual standard: 9 ug/m3 (revised 2024)
  EPA 24-hour standard: 35 ug/m3

  Ozone
  Parameter name: Ozone
  Sample duration: 8-HR RUN AVG BEGIN HOUR (8-hour running average)
  Regulatory standard applied: Ozone 8-hour 2015
  Units: Parts per million (ppm)
  EPA 8-hour standard: 0.070 ppm

Where multiple instruments (POC values) existed at a single site, the primary
instrument (lowest POC) was retained. Where multiple regulatory standards applied
to the same readings, the most current standard was retained. This produces one
row per monitoring station per pollutant.

DATA COVERAGE

Stations with an observation_pct below 75 are considered incomplete by EPA standards.
Their annual summary statistics may not reliably represent annual conditions and should
be interpreted with caution.

GEOGRAPHIC COVERAGE AND LIMITATIONS

Monitoring stations are fixed-location instruments. Areas of Cuyahoga County without
a nearby monitoring station may have substantially different air quality conditions than
what the monitored locations record. The spatial distribution of monitors is not random:
station placement reflects regulatory requirements, historical siting decisions, and
resource availability. Users should not assume that monitored locations are
representative of the county as a whole.

COLUMN DESCRIPTIONS

site_id ............. EPA monitoring site number (string; treat as identifier, not number)
local_site_name ..... Name assigned to the site by the operating agency
address ............. Street address of the monitoring station
latitude ............ Decimal degrees, WGS84
longitude ........... Decimal degrees, WGS84
parameter ........... Pollutant name (PM2.5 - Local Conditions or Ozone)
year ................ Calendar year (2024)
units ............... Units of measurement for concentration values
observation_pct ..... Percent of required monitoring days with valid observations
mean_value .......... Arithmetic mean of daily concentrations across the year
max_value ........... Highest single daily concentration recorded
pct_98th ............ 98th percentile of daily concentrations (used in PM2.5 design value)
pct_75th ............ 75th percentile of daily concentrations
pct_50th ............ Median (50th percentile) of daily concentrations
