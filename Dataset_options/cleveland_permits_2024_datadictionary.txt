Cleveland Building Permits — 2024
Frequently Asked Questions and Data Notes
City of Cleveland, Department of Building and Housing
Downloaded from: https://data.clevelandohio.gov
Accessed: June 15, 2026
File: cleveland_permits_2024.csv

----------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT IS THIS DATASET?

This file contains building permits filed with the City of Cleveland's Department of
Building and Housing during calendar year 2024. It has been filtered to include only
"New" category permits (new construction and significant renovations) with a status
of "Permit Closed," meaning the work was completed and the permit formally closed.

Each row is one permit. There are approximately 4,300 rows.

----------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT DOES EACH ROW REPRESENT?

One completed building permit filed in 2024. The permit covers a specific piece of
construction or renovation work at a specific address in Cleveland.

----------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT WAS EXCLUDED FROM THE ORIGINAL DATA?

The original dataset on the Cleveland Open Data Portal contains all permit types
(electrical, plumbing, HVAC, etc.) and all statuses (open, pending, closed). This
subset keeps only:
  - Permit type: Building Permit
  - Category: New (new construction and renovation; excludes electrical, plumbing, HVAC)
  - Status: Permit Closed (completed permits only)

Contractor names and license numbers were also removed to protect individual privacy.

----------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT SHOULD I WATCH OUT FOR?

Job values are self-reported. The job_value column contains the declared construction
value submitted by the permit applicant at time of filing. The city does not verify
these values before issuing the permit. Small jobs are frequently underreported,
and some applicants enter $0 or $1 as a placeholder. Do not treat this column as
a precise or verified measure of construction investment.

Issue dates can be in the future. Permits filed in late 2024 may show issue_date
values in 2025, reflecting normal processing and inspection time. This is not an
error; it is how the permitting process works.

Some neighborhoods are missing. Four permits have no neighborhood value (NA) due
to address geocoding failures. These rows are kept in the dataset rather than dropped.

This is city data only. The dataset covers the City of Cleveland municipal boundaries.
Suburban cities within Cuyahoga County (Parma, Lakewood, Shaker Heights, etc.) have
separate permit systems and are not included.

----------------------------------------------------------------

COLUMN REFERENCE

permit_id .......... Unique permit identifier assigned by city system (string)
year ............... Year permit was filed (2024)
file_date .......... Date permit application was submitted (YYYY-MM-DD)
issue_date ......... Date permit was issued or closed (YYYY-MM-DD; may be in 2025)
permit_subtype ..... Residential or Commercial
job_description .... Type of work as described by applicant (free text; not standardized)
job_value .......... Declared construction value in U.S. dollars (self-reported; see above)
total_fees_paid .... Permit fees paid to the city in U.S. dollars
ward ............... City council ward number (1-17; 1 NA value)
neighborhood ....... City of Cleveland neighborhood name (35 possible; 4 NA values)
latitude ........... Decimal degrees, WGS84 (1 NA value)
longitude .......... Decimal degrees, WGS84 (1 NA value)
