VARIABLES

date ..................... First day of reference month (YYYY-MM-DD)
year ..................... Calendar year (integer)
month ................... Month number, 1-12 (integer)
month_name .............. Three-letter month abbreviation
labor_force ............. Estimated civilian labor force (persons aged 16+, not military
                         or institutionalized)
employment .............. Estimated employed persons
unemployment ............ Estimated unemployed persons actively seeking work
unemployment_rate ....... Unemployment as percent of labor force (e.g., 8.8 = 8.8%)
data_flag ............... BLS revision status: "final" or "revised" (see Notes)

NOTES

These are model-based estimates, not direct counts. LAUS combines Current Population
Survey data, Current Employment Statistics data, and state unemployment insurance
records using a statistical model. Estimates are subject to revision.

Data are not seasonally adjusted. Unemployment rates are typically higher in winter
months and lower in summer months independent of broader economic conditions. For
trend analysis, year-over-year comparisons (same month, different years) are more
meaningful than month-to-month comparisons.

The April 2020 value (20.9%) reflects the acute impact of COVID-19 pandemic
restrictions. This represents a structural break in the series, not a typical
economic fluctuation.

The unemployment rate does not capture discouraged workers (those who have stopped
looking for work) or underemployed workers (part-time workers seeking full-time
employment).

data_flag values:
  "final"   -- Value has completed all BLS revision cycles.
  "revised" -- Value was updated through BLS annual benchmark revision, which
               incorporates additional administrative data. Revised values are
               more accurate than initial releases, not less reliable.
