DATASET: Millfield City Neighborhood Housing and Conditions
SOURCE: Fictional dataset created for Introduction to Data Science using Python (Chapter 3)
CITY: Millfield (fictional mid-sized U.S. city, ~180,000 residents)
UNIT OF ANALYSIS: Neighborhood (N=35)
TIME PERIOD: 2020-2023
CREATED BY: Course dataset, not real data. Patterns are illustrative.

KNOWN LIMITATIONS:
- All data are simulated. Neighborhood names, boundaries, and values are fictional.
- Equity patterns are designed to illustrate real-world disparities documented
  in housing research. They are not derived from any specific city's data.
- median_monthly_rent reflects median asking rent, not median rent paid.
  Rent-controlled or subsidized units may not be captured.
- code_violations_per_1000 reflects reported and inspected violations only.
  Neighborhoods with less frequent inspections may have undercounted violations.
- transit_score is a composite index (0-100) based on proximity to stops,
  frequency of service, and hours of operation. It is not derived from a
  standard published index.
- pct_broadband_access is based on household survey responses and may
  undercount households without internet access who were harder to reach.

VARIABLES:

neighborhood_id
  Type: string
  Description: Unique identifier for each neighborhood.
  Format: [district code]-[number] (N=North, E=East, W=West, S=South, DT=Downtown)
  Notes: Treat as a categorical identifier, not a number.

neighborhood_name
  Type: string
  Description: Common name for the neighborhood.

district
  Type: string (categorical)
  Description: City district the neighborhood belongs to.
  Valid values: North, East, West, South, Downtown

total_housing_units
  Type: integer
  Description: Estimated total number of housing units in the neighborhood.
  Units: Number of units

pct_nonwhite
  Type: float
  Description: Proportion of residents who identify as non-white.
  Units: Proportion (0.0 to 1.0). Multiply by 100 for percentage.

median_household_income
  Type: integer
  Description: Median annual household income for neighborhood residents.
  Units: U.S. dollars (nominal, 2022)

median_monthly_rent
  Type: integer
  Description: Median monthly asking rent for rental units in the neighborhood.
  Units: U.S. dollars per month
  Notes: Reflects asking rent, not necessarily rent paid. Does not include
         subsidized or rent-controlled units, which may be underrepresented
         in lower-income neighborhoods.

vacancy_rate
  Type: float
  Description: Proportion of housing units that are vacant.
  Units: Proportion (0.0 to 1.0). Multiply by 100 for percentage.
  Notes: Includes units vacant for any reason (for sale, for rent, seasonal,
         or other). High vacancy in low-income neighborhoods often reflects
         abandonment rather than market availability.

code_violations_per_1000
  Type: integer
  Description: Number of housing code violations cited per 1,000 housing units
               in the most recent inspection cycle.
  Units: Violations per 1,000 units
  Notes: Reflects reported and inspected violations only. Neighborhoods with
         less frequent code enforcement inspections may have undercounted
         violations regardless of actual housing conditions.

transit_score
  Type: integer
  Description: Composite index of public transit accessibility (0-100).
  Units: Index score (higher = better access)
  Notes: Based on proximity to stops, service frequency, and hours of
         operation. Not derived from a standard published index.

pct_broadband_access
  Type: float
  Description: Proportion of households with broadband internet access.
  Units: Proportion (0.0 to 1.0). Multiply by 100 for percentage.
  Notes: Based on household survey responses. May undercount households
         without internet access who were harder to reach by survey.

pct_owner_occupied
  Type: float
  Description: Proportion of occupied housing units that are owner-occupied.
  Units: Proportion (0.0 to 1.0). Multiply by 100 for percentage.
